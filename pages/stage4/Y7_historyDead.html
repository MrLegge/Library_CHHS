Mungo Man

Mungo man is perhaps one of the most important human remains ever found in Australia. Found at Lake Mungo, his skeleton is one of the oldest remains of modern humans found outside of Africa.

Otzi The Ice Man

Found by two hikers on the Italian-Austrian border in 1991, the ‘Iceman’ was to become the oldest, most prehistoric and well preserved mummy to be discovered ever. His skeleton was found half in ice, 3210 metres above sea level in the Ötztal Alps.

Tollund Man

Tollund man is a naturally mummified body. Found in 1950 buried in a peat bog in Denmark, Tollund man is one of the most well-known bog bodies. Since the body appeared so fresh, it was initially believed to be a victim of a recent murder, it was later discovered the mummy was from the 4th century BCE!

Lady Dai

Known also as Xin Hui, The Lady of Dai is a 2,100 year old mummy from China. Found in 1971, it is one of the best preserved ancient humans ever found. The body is so well preserved her skin is still soft and moist.

Ginger Egyptian

Discovered in 1896, ‘Ginger’ is one of the best preserved individuals from Ancient Egypt. Found in a shallow pit, he was naturally mummified by direct contact with the hot, dry sand.

King Tut

Tutankhamen or ‘King Tut’ is the famous boy king. Discovered in 1922, his burial tomb was one of the few discovered that still had everything in it. It contained priceless treasures and artefacts, as well as the king’s mummy lying in his solid gold coffin within a sarcophagus.

Lady of Oplontis

Human remain of a woman from the Mount Vesuvius eruption in 79 AD. This body has been preserved through the use of resin. Making it possible for future DNA study and other scientific research. Fiorelli had previously preserved the body through body casts which had kept the overall features but made it difficult for future study.

Peruvian Ice Maiden

Sacrificed five centuries ago, the Inca Ice Maiden is a well-preserved frozen body of an 11-15 year old girl. This 550 years old mummy was discovered in 1995 in southern Peru, has caused a sensation in the scientific world because of its well-preserved condition
