About the task:

Research an Australian scientist from the list. Check the task description and note-taking scaffold so you know what information is needed.

The Teacher Librarian will guide you during three lessons in the library. She will help you with locating and evaluating information, note-taking, bibliography writing and preparing your report.

Finish the task at home. Hand in both your Guided Inquiry Booklet and your 1-2 page report. Do your best by checking that your work matches the marking guidelines.

Australian Scientists - Disease

Frank Fenner was a pioneer of myxomatosis research in Australia and his research into 'mouse pox' led to key developments in general understanding of the pathogenesis of viral disease. Earl Owen

Australian Scientists - Microsurgery

Earl Owen was one of the earliest and most enterprising pioneers in the field of microsurgery. He caused considerable controversy after conducting the world's first successful finger replacement on a child. Later, he co-led the first successful hand transplant, trained the team that completed the first double-hand transplant, and completed the first reverse vasectomy and complete fallopian tube ligature.
